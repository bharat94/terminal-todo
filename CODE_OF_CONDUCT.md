# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in terminal-todo respectful,
welcoming, and free from harassment for everyone, regardless of experience,
identity, background, or ability.

## Expected behavior

- Be kind, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Assume good intent while taking responsibility for impact.
- Respect privacy, boundaries, and differing viewpoints.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about scope and community safety.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate
intimidation, doxxing, and sustained disruption are not acceptable.

## Scope and enforcement

This code applies in project repositories, issue trackers, reviews, community
spaces, and public interactions where someone represents the project.

Report conduct concerns privately through a
[private repository report](https://github.com/bharat94/terminal-todo/security/advisories/new)
with `[conduct]` at the start of the title. This uses GitHub's private
vulnerability-reporting channel as the project's current confidential inbox;
the report does not need to describe a vulnerability. If the concern involves
the sole maintainer, use [GitHub's abuse-reporting
process](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse) instead. Reports will
be handled as confidentially as practical. Maintainers may edit or remove
contributions and temporarily or permanently restrict participation when
behavior is harmful.

Enforcement decisions will consider context, impact, recurrence, and whether
the participant takes responsibility and changes their behavior.

This policy is inspired by the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).
